War Breaks Out
Above is the Christmas card sent out by my Mum and Dad in 1939. - Me reading the news. As a baby, Mum and Dad were very proud of their 'new addition' and in recognition of his arrival produced a Christmas card commemorating his arrival! Unlike me, said to be just like my father, he was a true Hussey! blonde hair blue eyes and the longest dark eye lashes ever seen in downtown West Wickham.
John F Pink
Here is John in the back garden of 49 Cuxton Road, Strood. On leave from his RAF days at RAF Halton.
To the right, JFP is celebrating one of his many birthdays!. (He had probably had about 62 by this time.) We were all at an OLD BEN Christmas party for the "Dicken's" committee and it coincided with his birthday.
By this time he had grown from what you see above, through WWII, left Woodlands Road school Gillingham to enter the RAF at Halton as an apprentice engine fitter, first on Hawker Hunters and ending up with treble one squadron and the world renowned 'Lightning' Seeing the world, and finishing working in the news trade in our family business.
