Computers
The Beginnings 1978 - 1980



Following our success with "Computernews" at the wholesale depot, in about 1978 I had bought a "PET COMPUTER" which stored all it's program on audio tapes, and held everything in it's 8kb memory until it was "saved" back to tape. I joined a night school class at Fort Horsted, and under the expert guidance from Steve Davis, learnt basic computer programming at a cost of £8.00 for the year!

It was not very long before I had transferred the magazine orders to a computer database which printed out the orders neatly for the user. We still worked from the paper sheets, but when they became un-useable through wear and tear, the computer would be up-dated and a fresh print-out obtained.

I had originally bought the computer to write a payroll program, because the cost of the computer was about £425 and we were paying a computer firm 2/6d. a week per pay record for something like 35 pay records. You could see that money could be saved by owning your own computer and writing your own software. I had a run in with the Inland Revenue over charging the computer to the firm. They said it would not be possible for a toy to used for a business, and business computers would cost many thousands of pounds. However, using the PET, I had written successfully a payroll for S/D which computed 135 pay records every week for the Dartford, Sheerness and Chatham branches. A job which took Mrs Jean Dorrington 40 hours to work out by hand. She was retiring and my program, once updated with any changes to overtime etc., ran and printed out payslips in about 20 minutes! complete with all analysis of departments, attachment to earnings etc., and correctly calculated the tax and NHI as per the tables. (I passed the formula to SAGE PAYROLL in later years)

Because of my interest in the computer scene I joined the Medway Computer and Robotics Club which met at Fort Horsted where I had attended my basic computer classes. It was run by enthusiast Paul Cameron, and he very ably demonstrated such things as data transfer via a serial bus and data storage and retrieval on various media of the day, - Before hard drives arrived on the scene. When the facility at the college ended we used to meet in his house in Walderslade with one or two die hards wielding Spectrum computers, Pet Computers, an Apple, a BBC Micro. - Possibly others. it was Pauls aim to write an adventure game that used straight forward "data files" so that each of us could design our own "game" and the data could be swapped between machines, what ever their operating system. -The project was never finished, although it was fun along the way.
